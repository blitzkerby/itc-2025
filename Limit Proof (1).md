$$
	\lim
		_{x \rightarrow 3}
			{(2x - 1)} 
		= 5
$$

**Scratch**
$$
\begin{align*}
	\left| 
		f(x) - L 
	\right|	
	&< \epsilon 
	
\\ \\
	\left| 
		(2x-1) - L 
	\right|	
	&< \epsilon
	
\\ \\
	\left| 
		2x-6
	\right|	
	&< \epsilon
	
\\ \\
	2
	\left| 
		x-3
	\right|	
	&< \epsilon
	
\\ \\
	\left| 
		x-3
	\right|	
		&< 
		\frac
			{\epsilon}
			{2}
		\quad = \quad
			\delta

\end{align*}	
$$

**Proof**
- Given $\epsilon > 0$,
- Set, $\delta = \frac{\epsilon}{2}$
- And note,
	if,
	 $|x-3| < \delta = \dfrac{\epsilon}{2}$
	then,
	 $\left| 2x - 6 \right| < \epsilon$
- Which implies,
	$$
		\left|  
			(2x - 1) - 5 
		\right| < \epsilon
	$$
- So,
$$
	\lim
		_{x \rightarrow 3}
		{(2x - 1)}
		= 5
$$
